Weighted $\ell_1$-minimization

In an upcoming work a convex optimization approach, based on weighted $\ell_1$-regularization, is proposed for reconstructing sparse wavelet representations. We show our proposed optimization problem is effective for solving the signal/image inpainting and denoising problems. We take the funcational representation of an image or signal to be $$ f(y) = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{J}} c_j \Psi_j(y) . $$ The wavelet coefficients $c = (c_j)_{j \in \mathcal{J}}$ associated to the functional representation of the object of interest are obtained as minizers of
$$ \min_{c \in \mathbb{R}^N} \lVert c \rVert_{\omega,1} + \lVert Ac - f \rVert_2, $$ where $f \in \mathbb{R}$ is either a vector $m$ subsamples or $m$ noisy observations.
We show that by choosing the weights to be the uniform norms of the wavelet functions, i.e., the $L^\infty$-norm of $\Psi_j$, the support of the recovered vector of coefficients forms a particular kind of index set, a closed tree.

An example of a closed tree. The solid edges form the closed tree.

This kind of index set is consistent with the behavior of many real-world signals and images and therefore our approach applies to a wide class of signals. Furthermore, the numerical examples below show the effectiveness of weighted $\ell_1$-minimization. In addition, we have shown that the sample complexity associated with the weighted $\ell_1$-regularized problem is smaller than the sample complexity of the unweighted problem. This analysis will appear in an upcoming paper.
